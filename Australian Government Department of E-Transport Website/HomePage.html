Tesla is an American multinational automotive and clean energy company. Tesla has earned a stout reputation over the past few

car to properly feature a self driving system that utilises satilite navigation to steer the car without driver input.


Tesla make a variety of E-car types such as:

    Tesla Model S: Is a luxury, high performance sports, sedan that can do 0-100km/h in just 1.9 seconds. This is made

    Tesla Model 3: Is more of an economical sedan that averages 576km per charge. The model 3 is significantly
    cheaper than the model S but still has many of the luxury features as the sports sedan variant.


    Tesla Model X: Is a luxury, small cross-over SUV. The model X averages 536km per charge, which is less than

    Tesla Cyber Truck: Is a luxury, high performance, off-road four-wheel drive truck. The truck has a range of 800km per
    charge, which makes it perfect for those long road trips.

With an electric delivery van that may not be far behind it.

Electric trucks

Volvo Trucks Australia has recently launched the all-electric medium duty truck range for Australian transport operators.
The new Volvo FL and FE Electric medium duty range meet the demand from society and customers for dramatic cuts in CO2 emissions.


    FL Electric: The Volvo FL Electric is built to make everyday life run even smoother with a minimal CO₂ footprint.
    It acts like the city's red blood cells - bringing what's needed to the right place, at the right time. The FL features up
    to 135kW of power with a range of up to 300km. It's key selling point is it's ability to haul 16 tonnes
    on pure electric motor.


    FE Electric: The Volvo FE Electric is larger than the FL as it has four entry points instead of two and a higher power motor
    of 225kW. As a result of it's larger size, the FE has a reduced range of 200km compared to the 300km that the FL offers.
    However, the FE has a much higher towing capacity of 23 Tonnes compared to 16 Tonnes from the FL.


Hauling loads with an electric truck goes a long way to help the environment. Diesel trucks are one of the highest polluting vehicles on the road.
If Australian companies are able to switch to electric trucks, it would cut emissions down massively. A big incentive is for companies to save
cost on fuel costs and maintenance repairs. Contact Volvo to find out more here: www.volvo.com.en

About the Australian Government Department of E-transport

Where are we located?

The Australian Government Department of E-transport is located in Parliament house, Canberra, ACT.
The department of E-transport is an online portal that is accessible for all web users from the Australian Government Department of Transport website.

How long have we been in operation for?

The Australian Government Department of E-transport has commenced operations in July 2022 with the rise in popularity in E-vehicles in Australia
in recent years. So yes, we are pretty new to the scene! With the rise of electric vehicles in Australia, the Department of transport is experiencing more
and more pressure to expand and create a sub department that specialises in EVs.

Don't worry, the Australian Government Department of E-transport isn't going anywhere! We plan to expand this website with more content and we always strive
to improve user experience.

What are our goals?

The Australian Government Department of E-transport has a primary goal to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by 43% below 2005 levels by 2030.

Carbon emissions from petrol and diesel vehicles are among the leading causes for global warming. This is why Australians need to make the switch to electric
vehicles in a timely manner. By choosing to drive an EV you are helping to reduce harmful air pollution from exhaust emissions. An EV has zero exhaust emissions,
but still creates a degree of greenhouse gas emissions when it is charged from the electricity grid.

Utilise renewable energy: If you have a solar PV system and charge your EV during the day, you can reduce your greenhouse gas emissions even further.
Another way is to purchase GreenPower from your electricity retailer. Then, even if you recharge your EV from the electricity grid, your electricity is coming
from renewable energy sources.

Improve the health of Australians: Reduced harmful exhaust emissions is good news for our health. Better air quality will lead to less health problems
and costs caused by air pollution3. EVs are also quieter than petrol/diesel cars, which means less noise pollution.

Improve the health of Australians: If EV charging is managed effectively, mainly outside peak electricity demand periods, it will help us to create a
flatter electricity network demand profile over a typical 24-hour period. This will help us to:

  • Better utilise the electricity network.
  • Help EV owners avoid higher-cost charging periods.
  • Help the entire electricity system work more efficiently.
  • Help support the integration of more small and large-scale renewable energy systems into the electricity grid.


On a national level, EVs can help with Australia’s liquid-fuel energy security. At present, Australia is highly dependent on other countries for petroleum imports.
EVs are easy to power from local and renewable energy sources, reducing our dependence on foreign oil. EV’s will be unaffected if fuel supplies to Australia are
ever disrupted or fuel costs increase significantly.
